What is Lung Care?

" Lung Care " is a mobile application that enables people to self-check for lung cancer. The application predicts the user's cancer chance as a percentage by analyzing the user's responses. Using this application, users will be able to quickly determine their cancer risk percentage. In addition, any queries a user may have concerning their cancer can be submitted to the community where the mobile application is used and will receive an official response in a very short amount of time. By sending CT scan photos to the app, users can obtain a full description of their particular cancer kind and stage in addition to the situations listed above.

P. Ramachandran and his colleagues used data mining to construct an early detection method for lung cancer that used 11 different characteristics [7]. They grouped the diagnostic indications based on age, gender, cancer family history, smoking, radiation, radon, chemical exposure, and air pollution exposure. Anorexia, a chronic cough, hemoptysis, chest pain, tiredness, weight loss, chronic lung inflammation, and wheezing, on the other hand, were discovered as indicators of malignancy. M. Markaki, I. Tsamardinos, and team members have established a clinical risk prediction model for lung cancer based on smoking symptoms [10]. Age, weight, height, hours spent in contaminated locations, frequency of coughing, sex, number of years of smoking, daily cigarette consumption, and years since stopping smoking were all considered. Cassidy concluded that it was preferable to explore other criteria other than smoking and age when constructing a robust lung cancer risk-prediction model [12]. Research Problem

The role of symptoms has not yet been comprehensively surveyed in detail, and therefore their impact on performance improvement has not been determined. This emphasizes symptom analysis and examines how the performance of classification based on symptom selection can be improved. This research makes it easier for patients to deal with and diagnose lung cancer by judging their symptoms and lifestyle using textual data. The focus of this analytical study is to pinpoint the key determinants that can cause or are key symptoms of lung cancer in various age groups to predict the percentage of having lung cancer in a person. According to the patient's symptoms, a pre-set question serious has been programmed. Predict output about the user's Lung cancer risk by evaluating the answers given by users. Expect relatively straightforward responses and also a user who already has scanned copies of their lung cancer can use our mobile application to learn more about and confirm their cancer status. The users can verify their status by simply uploading their images to the mobile application. In addition to the two scenarios described above, any questions users may have regarding their lung cancer can be posted in the community where the mobile application is used and receive a formal answer in a limited timeframe. The best thing about the application is that anyone can use the mobile application easily without prior knowledge. The image processing technology is used to diagnose lung cancers, Natural language and machine learning are used to predict the risk, and finally, the knowledgebase is used to store and classify questions

Objective

  • Use machine learning algorithm to predict lung cancer probability for a user by evaluating lung cancer symptoms.
  • The program is user-friendly and suitable for both technical and non-technical people.
  • Implement the system to make reliable predictions.​
  • Implement a user-friendly mobile app to keep track of patient’s cancer symptoms.​
  • Implement ontology-based integrated system knowledge for a specific domain that includes knowledge description and knowledge association.​
  • Lung cancer related question analyzing.​
  • Privacy detection and analyzing of the question.​
  • Patients can determine the extent of sickness in this section by utilizing image processing technologies. When a user uploads a CT scan, x-ray, or cell scan picture.​

Methodology

This research presents a mobile application built with the Flutter framework. The mobile application is compatible with both Android and iOS operating systems. This study is primarily divided into four sections, as follows:

  • Determine the cancer probability percentage using machine learning.
  • Analyze given inputs to the questionnaire and decide next question according to that.
  • Analyze the previous patient date according to the symptoms
  • Predict and display the probability percentage of the cancer.
  • If probability is high user can see how their nutrition and fitness plan should be changed.
